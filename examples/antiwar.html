The Evidence

SEE WHAT WAR
ACTUALLY LOOKS LIKE

Not the glory. Not the strategy. The raw, unfiltered truth of what human beings do to each other when we choose violence.

Into the Jaws of Death Omaha Beach, 1944 — Soldiers of the 1st Infantry Division wading ashore under fire. The first step on a path to liberate Europe.
Bloody Saturday Shanghai, 1937 — The bombing of a children's orphanage. War has no distinction between soldier and civilian. This image, taken by George Hatem, became one of the most haunting photographs of the 20th century.
Into No Man's Land Battle of the Somme, 1916 — Soldiers waiting in the trenches for the whistle that means certain death. 20,000 British soldiers died on the first day alone.
Valley of the Shadow of Death Crimean War, 1855 — Roger Fenton's iconic photograph of artillery balls lining the valley. The scene of devastation that words could never capture.
Napalm Girl Vietnam, 1972 — Nine-year-old Kim Phúc running naked down Highway 1, her body burned by napalm. This photograph helped turn the world against the war.
St Paul's Survives London Blitz, 1940 — The cathedral standing amid the flames. A symbol of resilience, and the devastating cost of a city under siege.
Victory Berlin, 1945 — The Soviet flag over the ruins of the Reichstag. Victory for some, devastation for all.
Just Like a Swarm of Flies Dachau, 1945 — General Eisenhower's words describing what American soldiers saw. The Holocaust: not just a war crime. An abyss of human cruelty that the world must never forget.
The Afghan Girl Sharbat Gula — Her green eyes defined an entire war. She became the symbol of the Afghan refugee crisis. For years, she lived in fear.
The Fall of Saigon 1975 — A tank crashes through the gates of the Presidential Palace. The end of a war, and the beginning of a new nightmare for millions.
The Highway of Death Gulf War, 1991 — Dozens of destroyed vehicles stretching to the horizon on Highway 80. The stark brutality of aerial bombardment.
Fireman Carries Boy Out of the Rubble London, 1940 — Amid the Blitz, a firefighter carries a sleeping child to safety. The people who rebuild are the ones who matter most.
"I have seen war. I have seen war take away men, brothers, friends. I have seen them eaten away by disease, and blown to bits by explosives, and burned to death behind tanks and armoured cars. I have seen children cry for their mothers. I have seen men weeping for God — for mercy."
— General George S. Patton
